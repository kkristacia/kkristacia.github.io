Semantic Segmentation of COVID-19 Infected Lung CT Images with DeepLabV3+

Author: Kristacia Kang

Radiological presentations of COVID-19 lung infection include ground-glass opacity (GGO), consolidations, and pleural effusions, visible from the lung computed tomography (CT) image. A radiologist maybe required not only to identify the types of infection presentations but also to estimate the severity of damage to the lungs. However, this is a manual time-consuming process which can overburned radiologists especially during high-load periods. An artificial intelligence (AI)-based model that can automatically and rapidly segments the different presentations of lung infections can direct radiologists to the relevant regions, reduce human error and improve patient triage, easing the burden on the overall health systems.

Objective

Develop a Deep Convolutional Neural Network (DCNN) model which can perform semantic segmentation on lung CT images on three presentation categories: GGO, consolidation, and pleural effusion.

Results

Predictions made by DeepLabV3+ based architectures with ResNet18 and MobileNetV2 backbones.

Methodology

The model was developed using transfer learning based on the DEEPLABV3+ architecture.

The method above works fine for small images, but for high-resolution images, it is prohibitively slow. The solution is to create an image pyramid, whereby the large image is blurred and downsampled multiple times. The image pyramid is then used for a coarse-to-fine search, whereby the deepest image in the pyramid (smallest and most blurry) is used to get a rough estimate for the alignment offset before searching on the larger images. Border Trimming

A border detection and trimming algorithm was implemented. The raw images contained either black or white borders, which can disrupt the alignment algorithm which relies on finding image similarity. Therefore, before processing the borders are detected and trimmed away. To find the borders, average pixels values along the rows and columns are calculated, and the borders are selected based on when a certain amount of consecutive pixel values are not within specified boundaries that represent complete black or white borders.
